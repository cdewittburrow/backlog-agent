## Overview

The tiebreaker is a short, self-contained interstitial that fires automatically after a voting round closes with equal vote counts. It replaces the results screen momentarily, runs a visible randomization animation, then reveals the winner and hands off cleanly to the existing results view. The experience should feel playful and conclusive — fate stepped in, and now it's decided.

## User flows

**Primary flow — tiebreaker triggered automatically:**
1. Voting round closes (organizer toggles voting off, or all participants have swiped)
2. App detects a tie between two or more options
3. Instead of showing results, the tiebreaker interstitial appears
4. Animation plays (coin flip for 2-way tie, spinning wheel / random draw for 3+ way tie)
5. Animation resolves to a single winner with a brief "Fate decided!" label
6. Winner card highlights; tiebreaker screen fades into the normal results view
7. Results view shows winner with a small "Decided by tiebreaker" badge

**Optional re-roll flow (nice to have):**
1. After animation resolves but before committing, a "Re-roll once?" prompt appears
2. Tapping it replays the animation with a new random seed
3. After one re-roll, the prompt disappears and the result is committed

**Multi-participant sync:**
- Tiebreaker outcome is computed server-side (Supabase edge function or a seeded random committed to the DB) to ensure all participants see the same winner
- All open participant sessions update simultaneously via Supabase realtime subscription

## Information architecture

No new navigation surfaces. The tiebreaker is a transient overlay/modal layered over the results screen — it does not require its own route unless deep-linking to results with tiebreaker state becomes necessary.

Tiebreaker outcome is persisted alongside the room record:
- `rooms` table: add `tiebreaker_triggered: boolean`, `tiebreaker_winner_option_id: uuid`
- Results view reads this flag to conditionally show the "Decided by tiebreaker" badge

## Interaction patterns

**Animation — 2-way tie (coin flip):**
- A large coin SVG flips in 3D (CSS `rotateY` animation), lands on heads or tails
- Heads = option A, tails = option B (assignment is random, shown briefly before flip begins)
- On landing, the coin fades and the winning option card slides in

**Animation — 3+ way tie (spinning wheel or random draw):**
- A simple slot-machine / roulette-style list scrolls through the tied option names
- Decelerates and locks on the winner
- Alternatively: a "drawing from a hat" animation with cards shuffling and one card pulled out

**Transition to results:**
- After animation, a checkmark or celebration micro-animation plays on the winner
- "Fate decided! 🎲" label appears below the winner name
- A "See full results" button or auto-redirect (after 2–3 seconds) moves to results view

**Organizer vs. guest experience:**
- Both see the same tiebreaker screen since the outcome is server-side
- Organizer sees the tiebreaker trigger immediately when they close voting; guests see it update via realtime subscription
- No separate notification needed — the screen change is the signal

## Edge cases + empty states

- **All options tied:** If every option has the same vote count (including zero), the tiebreaker still fires over all options — no special case needed.
- **Single option:** A single-option session can't tie; tiebreaker never triggers.
- **Guest joins after tiebreaker resolves:** Results view shows winner with tiebreaker badge; no animation replay.
- **Realtime connection drops during tiebreaker:** Client shows animation with a loading state until the server-committed winner is confirmed; if connection can't be restored, falls back to displaying a "Refresh to see result" message rather than showing a potentially incorrect local result.
- **Re-roll edge case:** Re-roll is only available once and only before the result is committed to the DB. If the user closes the app and re-opens, they see the committed result without a re-roll prompt.

4. Should the re-roll option be available to guests or only the organizer?
