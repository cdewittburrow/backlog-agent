## Problem
When reading through First Read, stories that have been read or skipped stay visible. There is no way to clear them out of the feed, which makes it hard to track what is new versus what has already been seen. The feed becomes cluttered and re-reading the same headlines wastes time.

## Goal
Let users dismiss stories as they scan, so the feed reflects only unread/unseen content. The key design decision is whether dismissal persists across sessions.

## Users
Chase, scanning headlines during limited reading windows (commute, lunch, etc.). The primary need is: "I already saw this, get it out of my way."

## Requirements

**Must have**
- A swipe gesture (mobile) or click/tap target (web) to dismiss a story
- Dismissed stories are hidden from the active feed view
- Dismissal persists for the current session at minimum

**Should have**
- Dismissal persists across sessions (survives app close/reload)
- Dismissed stories are marked, not deleted — they can be recovered
- Visual feedback on dismiss (e.g., swipe-away animation or fade)

**Nice to have**
- Bulk dismiss (e.g., "dismiss all above")
- "Show dismissed" toggle to recover stories

## Out of scope
- Syncing dismissed state across devices (v1)
- Dismissal affecting feed ranking or recommendation algorithms
- Per-source dismissal rules

## Open questions
- Is dismissal stored client-side (localStorage/AsyncStorage) or server-side?
- Does the app have user accounts / auth, or is it single-user?
- Should dismissed stories ever auto-expire (e.g., after 7 days)?
- What happens to a story that was dismissed and then the source re-publishes it with the same URL?

## Decision log
- Persistence across sessions preferred over session-only — the main use case is "I read this yesterday, do not show it again"
