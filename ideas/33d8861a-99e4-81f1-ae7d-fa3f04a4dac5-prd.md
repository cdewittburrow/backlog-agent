## Problem
The second view in First Read is the expanded state you reach after tapping a story card. Currently it surfaces only the lede — the same text already visible in the first-view card. This means expanding a story provides no new information, making the second view useless. Users who want more context before deciding to read the full article have nowhere to get it within the app.

## Goal
Define and implement a second view that gives users meaningfully more content than the first view, so they can make a read/skip decision without leaving the app. The constraint: the second view must work within the existing feed layout and cannot require scraping article pages — it must be built from what is already in the feed data.

## Users
Chase, scanning headlines in short windows. The second view serves the "I'm interested but not sure" moment — enough to decide whether to open the full article or dismiss.

## Requirements

**Must have**
- Second view shows content not already visible in the first view card
- If the feed provides more text beyond the lede (e.g., a full description field, extended summary, or additional paragraphs), display up to ~5 sentences or ~600 characters — enough to be useful, not so much it becomes a scroll
- Show article metadata: publication source name, author (if available in feed), and published date/time
- A clearly labelled "Read full article" action that opens the article URL in the device browser or an in-app web view
- Second view must not require loading additional network requests at expand time — content must already be fetched with the feed

**Should have**
- If no additional content is available beyond the lede, the second view still renders usefully: show source, date, author, and the "Read full article" button — do not just repeat the lede
- Estimated reading time if full article length can be inferred (optional, only if available from feed data)
- Smooth open/close animation that feels intentional, not abrupt

**Nice to have**
- Related stories from the same source surfaced at the bottom of the second view
- Share button to share the article URL

## Out of scope
- Fetching the full article content from the source URL (no scraping in v1)
- AI-generated summaries of the article
- Comments, reactions, or social features
- Saving articles for later (read-it-later / bookmarking)

## Open questions
- What fields does the current feed data model expose beyond headline and lede? Is there a `content` or `description` field that has more text?
- What is the current second-view component — is it a modal, a slide-up panel, a full-screen push, or an inline expansion?
- Does the app currently open articles in a browser or an in-app web view? Is there a preference?
- Are feed items timestamped consistently enough to show reliable published dates?

## Decision log
- Scoped strictly to feed-available content — no scraping — to keep this a data-display task, not a new data acquisition task
- Second view must earn its existence even when extra content is unavailable, by surfacing metadata rather than repeating the lede
